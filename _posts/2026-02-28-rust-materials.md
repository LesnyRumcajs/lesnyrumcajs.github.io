## Books

My preferred source of knowledge, especially in the era of AI slop cesspool. In general, I find O'Reilly and No Starch Press to be the best publishers for programming books. The publishers I didn't mention are not necessarily bad but, to my experience, they should be more picky with the books they publish and the quality of authors thy work with.

### Beginner-friendly

- [Programming Rust](https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/programming-rust-2nd/9781492052586/) by by Jim Blandy, Jason Orendorff, Leonora F. S. Tindall

- [Effective Rust](https://effective-rust.com/) by David Brysdale

- [Zero to Production in Rust](https://www.zero2prod.com/) by Luca Palmieri

### Intermediate and advanced

- [Rust for Rustaceans](https://rust-for-rustaceans.com/) by Jon Gjengset

- [Rust Atomics and Locks](https://mara.nl/atomics/) by Mara Bos


## YouTube channels

### Personal

- [Jon Gjengset](https://www.youtube.com/@jonhoo) - author of the [Rust of Rustaceans](https://rust-for-rustaceans.com/) book and the [Crust of Rust](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLqbS7AVVErFiWDOAVrPt7aYmnuuOLYvOa) YouTube series.

- [Logan Smith](https://www.youtube.com/@_noisecode)

### Conference recordings

Going to conferences and meeting fellow Rustaceans is great, but personal travel budgets are limited. Watching the talks on YouTube is the next best thing (plus it's easier to escape from a boring talk, speed-up slow speakers and re-watch the good ones).

- [EuroRust](https://www.youtube.com/@EuroRust)

- [Rust Foundation](https://www.youtube.com/@rustfoundation) - RustConf, Rust Global, and more.

- [Rust Videos](https://www.youtube.com/@RustVideos) - also RustConf, plus some other events and talks.

- [Rustikon 2025](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QrbkG2I3RPo&list=PL8NC5lCgGs6MnMLJvhFH2jLa8bLYwCzmI)

- [RustLab](https://www.youtube.com/@rustlabconference3671)
